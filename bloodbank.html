Save Lives

    • Blood Group A

      Blood group A individuals have A antigens on the surface of their red blood cells and B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from A and O blood groups and can donate to A and AB blood groups.

    • Blood Group B

      Blood group B individuals have B antigens on the surface of their red blood cells and A antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from B and O blood groups and can donate to B and AB blood groups.

    • Blood Group AB

      Blood group AB individuals have both A and B antigens on the surface of their red blood cells and no A or B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from A, B, AB, and O blood groups and are known as universal recipients.

    • Blood Group O

      Blood group O individuals have no A or B antigens on the surface of their red blood cells and both A and B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood only from O blood group and are known as universal donors.
